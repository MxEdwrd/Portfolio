Projects

Max has worked on many different projects throughout the years. Below are a few of the big ones he has worked on!

MaxLeg

In February of 2020, I was hit by a stop-sign runner on my motorcycle. This accident had shattered my right ankle and broke my right tibia and fibula. For the next 6 months, I was laid up in bed and unable to walk or use my right leg. This had motivated me to design and create the MaxLeg.

The Maxleg is a brace-like device that attaches to the knee and lower leg and allows mobility on a leg that would otherwise not be able to be walked on. It is interchangeable from right to the left leg so it can be used on either. Most of the device is made of 3D printed plastic, however, there are places where PVC and old shoe sole were used as they proved to provide better strength and comfort.

I had used this brace until I was able to walk on my leg like normal and start physical therapy. This device had proven to be a fantastic design and had helped me heal happier with mobility. Not only was it a great apparatus, but it had gotten the attention of lots of people and spread the word of my design skills.

MaxMask

With the spread of COVID-19 and the beginning of masks becoming widely worn and needed, I was hired by a previous mentor of mine to design and create a mask design that would allow maximum protection from outside elements while still allowing for high breathability. This was the start of the MaxMask.

The first designs of the MaxMask were very rough and abstract. I had never designed a mask before and one that had to conform to the human face - a very complex and varying surface - was quite a challenge. The first designs looked like a part of a hazmat suit or were too flimsy to hold up well and required sewing in order to attach the filter material to the mask shell.

However, after weeks of work and design changes, I had come up with a design that conformed well to the human face while also allowing for maximum breathability. With that, the MaxMask was created. We had sent these masks with filters to healthcare workers and local clinics who were not able to get new masks during the shortage and had to reuse dirty surgical masks.
